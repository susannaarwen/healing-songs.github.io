Healing Songs

Intro

Have you ever noticed the healing power of music? 'Course you have.

Have you ever noticed that songs have the power to heal us?

Have you ever had that feeling that there is only one song, and only one song in the whole wide world, that could help you out right now? I have had this feeling millions of times.

So without further ado here are some of the songs that have helped me to heal in my life.

They have come to me at different time periods, but they have always been there along the way

What will you find on this list of healing songs?

Have fun!

Blue Banisters

Lana del Rey

From her 2021 album of the same name, Blue Banisters by Lana del Rey is one of the softest, gentlest, most tender songs that I have ever heard.

My favourite part of the song is at the very end of the song.

''Summer comes,
Winter goes,
Spring I skip,
God knows.''

And all of the bits after that.

For me this song is all about acceptance.

Paradise

Coldplay

This song came out when I was 18. I wish that you could know how I was feeling. This song makes me feel like I could do anything in the world. Kind of like in the previous song, Blue Banisters, this song is all about acceptance.

With the line 'She said, oh, I know the sun must set to rise', I feel there is a kind of acceptance.

This song just speaks to the dreamer in me.

It is such a feelgood song, the ultimate one!

Imagine just finishing school and having this playing all the time.

It was the best thing ever, oh my god.

Biking Home

Lisa Gerrard

This song represents to me alignment. Being true to yourself and following your destiny.

Now what could possibly be more healing than that?

There are no lyrics in this song, but there are some lines of dialogue in Maori at the end

The reason for this being, this is from the soundtrack of one of my favourite films, the Whale Rider!!!

If you have not seen this film, then I highly highly recommend it. Check it out!

Womankind

Emily Jane White

This is honestly one of the most healing songs that I have ever heard in life. It was actually my most played song of 2022!

The day I discovered it I just played this song for hours and hours on end. It articulated so many of the things I have never known how to say

But it's so empowering as well!

There is power in being understood and being validated. Ulaimately, that is what this song did to me. It put into words a lot of things that I could never say.

'Our body's not a public space
Corporal autonomous space
Hands that enter your space
They violate the whole human race.'

Yosemite

Lana Del Rey

This is the most healing song of all! It is so soft, and so gentle, and so magical, and mysterious.

If you've been following my coding journey, you'll know that I consistently quote and reference this song. The reason for this is that Lana del Rey sings about doing things 'for the right reasons'. And I fundamentally want to believe that I am doing coding for the right reasons: that I am doing it for the good of the whole.

"We did it for fun, we did for for free
From when I was young, 'til eternity
We did it for the right reasons
Withstanding all of time, changes and seasons
Right reasons."

Venice Bitch

Lana Del Rey

I've been battling internally with myself whether to keep this in or not, but I finally decided to put it back in.

This is a beautiful, healing song. It got me through a couple of nights where I just really needed to do some healing and to keep going.

It'd a 10 minute song!!! Can you believe it? For Lana del Rey.

It's a very progressive rock song with psychadelic undertones

But ultimately yes, I love how this song progresses from one thing to another. It starts off in one place and it ends up in a completely different place.

As one fan said on YouTube, "As long as this song exists, summer will never end."

Slow Burn

Kacey Musgraves

For me, this is one of the most healing songs ever.

The softness, the gentless, the stillness, that is encompassed in this song, for me is just on another level.

And everybody that I recommend it to falls in love with it too!

But this is what happened to me as well you see. One of my trainers recommended it to me in my very first job, when I was working in customer services

Ultimately, this song is what got me through the training, which was very very very hard and very difficult for someone who is neurodivergent!

My favourite part in this song is just when she goes 'Mmmh-mmmh-mmmh-mmmh-mmmh-mmmmh' at the start of the 3rd verse.
So soft.
So powerful.
And so gentle.

Speed of Sound

Coldplay

Yes, I know, it's so embarassing.

Please don't disown me!

But I love this song so, so, so much!

For me, it is the ultimate feel-good song.

In fact, when I was little, then I just used to think of it as the 'ultimate' song. Just like I used to think of English as the 'ultimate' - language - although I have spoken many languages since I was very young, I used to think of English as the one which had all the correct form etc. (lol). In the same way, I used to think of this opening riff as 'THE BEST SONG EVER'.

With all other songs merely aspiring to that.

Bonus Song: Bollicine

Vasco Rossi

Picture the scene.

It is a beautiful late autumn evening. There is a full moon.

I have just been to the pub with my colleagues. We had a great night, but I stayed on dancing. They all left.

So naturally I made friends with a group of about 8 Italians, the only other enthusiastic dancers in the pub.

I decided to go back to their after party.

As we walked back in the moonlight across 7 Dials in Brighton, the boy I was walking with put this song on.

He changed my life forever. I love this song.
